Program Overview

Train like a speed athlete, not just a gym athlete.

This program combines sprinting, plyometrics, lower-body strength, tendon stiffness, hip power, core stability, hip abductor control, and football-specific agility.

⚡

Acceleration

Short sprints, hill sprints, resisted runs, and first-step drills build explosive takeoff speed.

🏃

Top Speed

Flying sprints, sprint mechanics, and full recovery help improve maximum velocity.

🏋️

Strength

Squats, deadlifts, hip thrusts, calf raises, and Nordics build the force needed for speed.

🦵

Achilles Tendon

Calf raises, isometric holds, pogos, and elastic jumps improve ankle stiffness and ground contact speed.

🧱

Core Stability

Anti-rotation, planks, carries, and trunk control help transfer force without wasting movement.

↔️

Hip Abductors

Lateral band walks, cable abductions, and side plank variations improve pelvis control and cutting stability.

🔁

Agility

Shuttle runs, reaction sprints, curved runs, and ball sprints build football-specific movement.

🛡️

Injury Resistance

Hamstring, groin, Achilles, core, and hip stability work reduce common sprint-related weak links.

Weekly Schedule

Your updated weekly training structure

The week is organized to balance sprint quality, strength development, core stability, Achilles tendon loading, hip abductor strength, and recovery.

Monday

Acceleration + Strength + Achilles

Short sprints, hill work, squats, RDLs, hip thrusts, calf and Achilles work.

Tuesday

Core + Mobility + Hip Abductors

Core stability, hip control, glute medius, groin balance, and tibialis work.

Wednesday

Max Speed + Plyometrics + Achilles

Flying sprints, pogos, bounds, jumps, ankle stiffness, and tendon reactivity.

Thursday

Recovery + Light Core

Rest, mobility, breathing work, light trunk stability, and easy ball work.

Friday

Strength + Power + Core

Deadlifts, front squats, power cleans, Nordics, carries, and trunk stiffness.

Saturday

Football Speed + Abductors

Reaction sprints, shuttles, curved runs, ball sprints, and lateral hip strength.

Sunday

Rest

Full recovery to allow your body to adapt, rebuild, and get faster.

Every Session Warm-Up

Complete this warm-up before sprinting or lifting. It prepares your hips, hamstrings, calves, Achilles tendon, ankles, trunk, and nervous system.

  • Light jog5 min
  • Leg swings front/back2 × 10 each
  • Leg swings side/side2 × 10 each
  • Walking lunges2 × 10 each
  • A-skips2 × 20 m
  • High knees2 × 20 m
  • Butt kicks2 × 20 m
  • Ankling / pogos2 × 20 m
  • Lateral band walk1 × 10 each side
  • Dead bug activation1 × 8 each side
  • 60% sprint build-up2 × 30 m
  • 80% sprint build-up2 × 30 m

      Testing Progress

      Measure your speed before and after the program

      Test in Week 0 before starting, then repeat after Week 8. Use the same surface, shoes, and timing method.

      10 m Sprint

      Measures first-step explosion and early acceleration.

      30 m Sprint

      Measures football burst speed and acceleration power.

      Flying 20 m

      Measures top speed once already moving.

      Broad Jump

      Measures horizontal lower-body power.

      5-10-5 Shuttle

      Measures change of direction and re-acceleration.

      Single-Leg Balance Reach

      Measures hip abductor control, knee control, and pelvis stability.

      Pogo Contact Quality

      Measures Achilles stiffness, ankle reactivity, and elastic bounce.

      Core Hold Test

      Measures trunk stiffness and ability to maintain sprint posture.

      Training Rules

      Progress safely and stay explosive

      Weight Progression

      Too easy Add 2.5–5 kg next session
      Good but challenging Keep the same weight
      Form breaks Reduce 5–10 kg
      Pain or sharp tightness Stop the exercise

      Speed Priorities

      Glutes Hamstrings Calves Achilles Tendon Hip Flexors Quads Core Hip Abductors Adductors Tibialis

      Do not chase fatigue. Sprint quality matters more than doing more reps. Stop if your hamstrings, Achilles, groin, knees, hips, or lower back feel unsafe.
